One mile at a time

A story about running
by
Marcus Ta

The Start

When did I first get into running?

In Auguest of 2014, with zero race experiance and improper traning I completed my first half marathon

I soon found this atheletic sport something I wanted to do. The feeling of passing the finish line was something that I've never experianced before.

13.1 miles later I was already ready to sign up for my next race. This was just the start of a hobby I soon fell in love with.

What was next?

The goal to Complete a 1/2 Marathon under 1hr:45 minutes

Soon after my first race, I decided to sign up for mulitple 10ks (6.2 mile races) in hopes to better improve myself as runner. About one year later, I signed up for the Redmond Labor Day Half Marathon.

With the hopes to achieve my goal, unfortunately I fell short, running a 1hr 47 minute race. Dissapointed in myself, it took me 2 more race atempts till I got a PR (personal record) at the end of 2015.

What i learned from that experiance was, you will get the results based on how much effort you put in. Persistance is key and so is hard work.

Races I completed from 2014-2015

  1. Disneylannd Half Marathon
  2. Dawg Dash 10k
  3. Lake Union 10k
  4. Seattle Rock n Roll Half Marathon
  5. Disneyland Half Marathon

Soon after achompishing my PR, I then decided to double the distance and run the Marathon

The Marathon

2 years after my first race, I signed up for the Walt Disney World Marathon.

In January 2016, I completed the Walt Disney World Marathon and would later complete the Seattle Rock n Roll Full Marathon in June. Ever since then I have ran the Walt Disney World Marathon and Dopey Challenge every year.

The Disney World Marathon offers a challenge called The Dopey Challenge, this a 4 day race event where you must complete a :

  1. 5k
  2. 10k
  3. Half-Marathon
  4. Marathon
This was one of the hardest challenges I had to face, but the most satisfying of them all.

  6. New York Marathon (November 2019)

The Finish Line

What I learned from my few years of running, is that even when you past that finish line. The race is just getting started.

Running is not for everyone, but it is a good form of exercise that is free and can be practiced from any skill range and any age.

It taught me many life skills in which I use everyday and how strong the mind can be once you push your limits.
